Albert Camus, born 7 November 1913 in Mondovi near Bone, Algeria, and died on January 4, 1960 in Villeblevin in the Yonne in France, 1 is a writer, philosopher, novelist, playwright, essayist and French novelliste. He is also a journalist activist involved in the French Resistance and close libertaires2 currents in the moral struggles of the postwar period.
